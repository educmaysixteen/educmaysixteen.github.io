FOOD TECHNOLOGY

INTRODUCTION

This four-year program equips students with necessary competencies in the scientific handling and processing of foods, both in commercial and small-scale industries. The holistic formation of students includes the development of proper work and research ethics.

Asst. Prof. Elizabeth H. Arenas, Ph.D.

PROGRAM GOALS

1.Provide future food technologists with adequate and functional theoretical and practical know-how and understanding of food processing operations.

2.Expose the students to recent trends and advances in food science and technology that facilitate effective food processing operations, responsive to industry and community needs.

3.Establish and maintain favorable local, national, international linkages for purposes of research collaboration, information updates, and technology exchange.

4.Develop Food Technologists endowed with Christian values who are ready to serve the food needs, problems and concerns of target communities.

PROGRAM INTENDED LEARNING OUTCOMES

1. Describe the nexus between food technology and other disciplines.
2. Discuss the roles and importance of food technologists in society.
3. Discuss the fundamental principles underlying various processing and preservation methods applicable to major food commodities.
4. Identify parameters that define quality of raw material suitable for processing.
5. Explain basic chemistry of macro- and micro- molecules present in foods.
6. Discuss how food chemical components affect food quality and shelf life.
7. Discuss the thermal and non-thermal technologies used in the preservation of foods of plant and animal origins.
8. Match the identified quality of material with appropriate processing method.
9. Describe different types of packaging materials and methods.
10. Discuss product shelf life determination.
11. Discuss the principles and methods of chemical, physic-chemical, microbiological and sensory assessment of foods.
12. Apply the principles of fluid mechanics, thermodynamics and heat transfer in solving food problems on food engineering processes.
13. Apply appropriate methods in determining food quality via objective and subjective evaluations.
14. Discuss principles and methods of food safety and sanitation.
15. Apply the basic principles of food safety and sanitation.
16. Comply with food laws and regulations.
17. Describe current trends and issues in solving problems related to food science and technology.
18. Organize seminars on the current issues on food science and technology.
19. Critique relevant journal articles in food science and technology.
20. Write a research proposal consistent with the standards and rigor of scientific research.
21. Conduct research on emerging topics on food science and technology.
22. Disseminate relevant research findings via oral and mural forms.
23. Participate in actual operations in food practicum sites.
24. Document operations and practices observed in food practicum sites.
25. Work collaboratively with food industry practitioners in carrying out various food processing operations.
26. Involve themselves actively in community development programs, projects and activities that call for the use and application of their food science and technology know-how.
27. Identify the food needs, problems and concerns of target communities.
28. Initiate worthy community development projects and activities concerning the promotion of food quality and safety.
